## Introduction

## Quick Win #1: Menu Pricing Optimization

### The 3-Number Test

**Problem:** Most restaurants underprice their menu without realizing it.

**Solution:** Run the 3-Number Test on your top 10 menu items

#### How to Do It:

□ **Step 1:** Calculate your actual food cost for each item
   - Include every ingredient, garnish, and accompaniment
   - Don't forget cooking oil, seasoning, and waste

□ **Step 2:** Calculate your ideal food cost percentage
   - Industry standard: 28-32% for full-service, 25-30% for fast-casual
   - Your target: ___________% (write it down)

□ **Step 3:** Run the math
   - Current Price: $__________
   - Food Cost: $__________
   - Food Cost %: $__________ (Food Cost ÷ Price × 100)
   - Ideal Price: $__________ (Food Cost ÷ Target % × 100)

□ **Step 4:** Identify pricing gaps
   - Items priced too low: __________________
   - Items priced correctly: __________________
   - Items that need adjustment: __________________

#### Quick Action:

□ Increase prices on 3-5 underpriced items by $0.50-$2.00
□ Test the change for 2 weeks and monitor sales volume
□ Adjust again if needed

**Expected Impact:** 2-5% increase in profit margins with minimal customer pushback

## Quick Win #2: Labor Cost Reduction (Without Cutting Staff)

### The Schedule Optimization Strategy

**Problem:** Many restaurants are overstaffed during slow periods and understaffed during rushes.

**Solution:** Align labor hours with revenue hours

#### How to Do It:

□ **Step 1:** Pull sales data for the last 4 weeks
   - Break down revenue by hour and day of week
   - Identify your peak hours: __________________
   - Identify your slow hours: __________________

□ **Step 2:** Calculate your current labor cost %
   - Total Labor Cost: $__________
   - Total Revenue: $__________
   - Labor Cost %: $__________ (Labor ÷ Revenue × 100)
   - Industry target: 25-35% depending on service style

□ **Step 3:** Right-size your schedule
   - **Cut 30 min - 1 hour** from shifts during slow periods
   - **Add staff** during peak hours (if needed)
   - **Cross-train** staff to work multiple positions

□ **Step 4:** Implement shift rules
   - No double coverage during off-peak unless revenue justifies it
   - Stagger start times to match customer flow
   - Send staff home early when it's dead (with their permission)

#### Quick Action:

□ Adjust next week's schedule based on revenue patterns
□ Track labor cost % daily for 2 weeks
□ Tweak as needed to hit your target

**Expected Impact:** 2-4% reduction in labor costs without sacrificing service quality

## Quick Win #3: Inventory Waste Prevention

### The Weekly Count System

**Problem:** Restaurants lose 4-10% of food cost to waste, theft, and over-portioning.

**Solution:** Implement a simple weekly inventory tracking system

#### How to Do It:

□ **Step 1:** Set up a tracking sheet
   - List your top 20 highest-cost ingredients
   - Track weekly: Starting Inventory + Purchases - Ending Inventory = Usage
   - Compare usage to sales (e.g., if you sold 100 burgers, you should have used ~100 patties)

□ **Step 2:** Identify waste sources
   - **Over-portioning:** Staff using too much per dish
   - **Spoilage:** Items expiring before use
   - **Theft:** Unexplained inventory shrinkage
   - **Prep error:** Incorrect recipes or measurements

□ **Step 3:** Implement controls
   - **Portion tools:** Scoops, ladles, scales for consistency
   - **FIFO system:** First In, First Out for all ingredients
   - **Waste log:** Track what gets thrown away and why
   - **Recipe cards:** Posted at every station

□ **Step 4:** Count and compare weekly
   - Actual usage: $__________
   - Expected usage: $__________
   - Variance: $__________ (highlight anything over 5%)

#### Quick Action:

□ Do a full inventory count this week as your baseline
□ Train staff on proper portioning with tools
□ Start logging waste daily

**Expected Impact:** 2-3% reduction in food cost within 30 days

## Quick Win #4: Peak Hour Revenue Maximization

### The Upsell Strategy

**Problem:** Restaurants leave money on the table by not maximizing average check size during busy periods.

**Solution:** Train staff on strategic upselling techniques

#### How to Do It:

□ **Step 1:** Calculate your current metrics
   - Average check size: $__________
   - Average covers per day: __________
   - Target check increase: $__________ (even $2-3 per check adds up)

□ **Step 2:** Identify upsell opportunities
   - **Appetizers:** "Can I start you with our [signature appetizer]?"
   - **Drinks:** "Would you like to try our [seasonal cocktail]?"
   - **Upgrades:** "Want to add [premium protein] for just $X more?"
   - **Desserts:** "Save room for our [best-selling dessert]?"

□ **Step 3:** Create an upsell playbook
   - Train staff on 3-5 specific upsell phrases
   - Focus on high-margin items (drinks, appetizers, desserts)
   - Make it easy: "Most guests love our _____"

□ **Step 4:** Track and reward
   - Measure average check size daily
   - Recognize top performers
   - Create friendly competition with small incentives

□ **Step 5:** Strategic menu placement
   - Feature high-margin items prominently
   - Use descriptive language that sells
   - Add photos for visual appeal

#### Quick Action:

□ Hold a 15-minute pre-shift meeting to teach 2-3 upsell phrases
□ Track average check for next 2 weeks
□ Adjust and refine based on what works

**Expected Impact:** $2-5 increase in average check size = 10-25% revenue boost

## Quick Win #5: Customer Retention Tactics

### The Repeat Visit System

**Problem:** It costs 5x more to acquire a new customer than to retain an existing one, yet most restaurants don't have a retention strategy.

**Solution:** Build a simple system to bring customers back

#### How to Do It:

□ **Step 1:** Capture customer contact info
   - Email/phone at checkout or reservation
   - WiFi signup form
   - Loyalty program enrollment

□ **Step 2:** Create a welcome sequence
   - **Email 1 (Day 1):** Thanks for visiting, here's 10% off your next visit
   - **Email 2 (Day 7):** Share a behind-the-scenes story or chef's special
   - **Email 3 (Day 14):** Invite them back with a limited-time offer

□ **Step 3:** Segment by behavior
   - **First-time visitors:** Welcome sequence
   - **Regulars:** VIP perks and early access to specials
   - **Lapsed:** "We miss you" win-back offer

□ **Step 4:** Implement a loyalty program
   - **Simple:** Buy 9, get the 10th free (punch card or digital)
   - **Points-based:** Earn points per dollar spent
   - **VIP tier:** Exclusive perks for top customers

□ **Step 5:** Ask for feedback
   - Send a post-visit survey via email
   - Respond to all reviews (good and bad)
   - Fix problems quickly to prevent churn

#### Quick Action:

□ Start collecting emails/phones TODAY (put a signup sheet at checkout)
□ Set up a simple email sequence (use free tools like Mailchimp)
□ Send first "thank you" email to new customers within 24 hours

**Expected Impact:** 15-30% increase in repeat visit rate within 60 days
